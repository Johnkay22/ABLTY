REALITY CHECK
Perceptual training for lucid dreaming
01 / 05 - How It Works
🌙
Lucid Dream Training
To have a lucid dream you need to recognize you're dreaming while it's happening. The obstacle: your dreaming mind accepts everything as real by default.

Reality checks train the habit of deliberate awareness. Do them enough while awake and your sleeping mind eventually asks the same question - and catches the answer.
02 / 05 - The Pager
📳
Random Triggers
The checks are sent at random intervals throughout your waking hours - not on a fixed schedule. Randomness is key. If you knew when to expect them, you'd anticipate them instead of genuinely checking.

Each notification opens a short micro-task. The whole thing takes under 30 seconds.
03 / 05 - Physical Check
👃
Nose Pinch Test
Pinch your nose shut and try to breathe through it. In waking life: impossible. In a dream: you can still breathe.

This is one of the most reliable checks available because it requires no subjective judgment - the result is binary and unmistakable once you're trained to test for it.
04 / 05 - Mental Checks
🧠
Location & Strangeness
Location memory: Dreams rarely have coherent origin stories. Ask yourself how you got here. If you can't trace it, that's a signal.

Strangeness rating: Dream environments shift and distort in subtle ways. Rating your surroundings on a 1-5 scale trains you to notice when something's off.
05 / 05 - Evening Primer
✦
Pre-Sleep Suggestion
30 minutes before your set bedtime, the pager sends a final notification with a presleep intention.

This targets the hypnagogic transition - the window between wakefulness and sleep where suggestion is most effective. It reinforces the habit right before your mind enters the state you're training for.
