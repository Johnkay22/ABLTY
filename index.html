REALITY CHECK
Perceptual training for lucid dreaming
Why This Works
Lucid dreaming requires recognizing you're in a dream while it's happening. The obstacle is that your dreaming mind mirrors your waking habits — it accepts whatever it encounters as real.

Reality checks work by training a habit of deliberate perception. Ask "am I dreaming?" enough times while awake and your sleeping mind will eventually ask the same question — and notice when the answer is yes.

The checks in this app target the four most reliable dream indicators:
01
Location memory. Dreams rarely have coherent origin stories. If you can't explain how you got somewhere, that's a signal.
02
Environmental strangeness. Dream environments shift and distort. Rating your surroundings trains you to notice when something is off.
03
Nose pinch test. In dreams, you can breathe through a pinched nose. This is one of the most reliable physical checks available.
The evening primer — sent 30 minutes before your set bedtime — plants a suggestion that reinforces dream awareness during the transition to sleep.
