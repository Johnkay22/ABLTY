The Research
THIS IS REAL SCIENCE
For over two decades, the U.S. government funded classified research into human psi abilities. The results were significant enough to run the program for 23 years. That research is now declassified - and the methodology is the foundation of ABLTY.
Declassified · CIA · 1995
Project STARGATE
A 23-year, $20 million U.S. government program at Stanford Research Institute investigating remote viewing. Declassified in 1995. Researchers Hal Puthoff and Russell Targ demonstrated statistically significant results across thousands of trials. Operated under the Defense Intelligence Agency.
Peer-Reviewed · Journal of Scientific Exploration
Meta-Analysis of Remote Viewing Experiments
A meta-analysis of 653 remote viewing trials across multiple laboratories found an overall hit rate significantly above chance (p < 0.0001). Effect sizes were consistent across independent research groups. Utts, J. (1996).
Princeton University · PEAR Lab · 1979-2007
Princeton Engineering Anomalies Research
28 years of rigorous laboratory research at Princeton University examining human remote perception. Over 80 technical reports published. The remote perception database includes 650+ formal trials demonstrating consistent anomalous cognition effects.
Peer-Reviewed · Psychological Bulletin · 1994
Ganzfeld ESP Studies
A meta-analysis of 11 independent Ganzfeld databases (2,549 sessions) found a hit rate of 33.2% against a 25% chance baseline. A statistically significant result replicated across independent laboratories. Bem, D. & Honorton, C.
